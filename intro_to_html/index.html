Giant Anteater Facts

Description and Size

From nose to tail, giant anteaters can reach up to 8 feet in length. They have a distinctive, long snout with a two foot long tongue. Giant anteaters do not have any teeth. The fur on the giant anteater resembles a horse's mane and is long and bushy.

Habitat

Giant anteaters are present in a majority of Central and South America. However, they are thought to be extinct in El Salvador, Guatemala, and Uruguay. These anteaters prefer wetlands, grasslands, and tropical forests.
